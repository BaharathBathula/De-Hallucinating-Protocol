# Escalation Flow Architecture

---

# Overview

The Escalation Flow Architecture defines how governance escalation propagates through enterprise AI execution workflows.

The architecture is designed to ensure that high-risk operational states remain continuously governable through deterministic escalation orchestration.

The framework treats escalation as operational governance infrastructure rather than reactive exception handling.

---

# Core Philosophy

Traditional AI systems frequently escalate only after confidence degradation becomes visible.

De-Hallucinating Protocol instead treats escalation as a continuously evaluable governance mechanism.

Escalation decisions may activate due to:

- governance instability
- policy conflicts
- trust degradation
- retrieval anomalies
- authority inconsistencies
- operational ambiguity
- regulatory sensitivity

Escalation is treated as runtime governance orchestration.

# Escalation Flow Stages

## 1. Governance Event Detection

The protocol continuously monitors for governance-relevant events.

Examples include:

- trust degradation
- policy conflicts
- hallucination indicators
- retrieval instability
- operational anomalies
- escalation boundary violations

Objective:

Detect governance instability before uncontrolled execution occurs.

---

## 2. Trust Reevaluation

The trust engine reevaluates:

- runtime trust states
- governance confidence
- operational reliability
- escalation necessity

Objective:

Determine whether execution remains governable.

---

## 3. Policy Severity Analysis

The governance engine evaluates:

- policy sensitivity
- compliance exposure
- execution restrictions
- operational impact
- escalation severity

Objective:

Determine escalation urgency.

---

## 4. Governance State Classification

The protocol classifies runtime execution into governance states:

- Trusted
- Monitored
- Restricted
- Escalated
- Blocked

Objective:

Determine operational governance posture.

---

## 5. Escalation Routing

The escalation engine routes execution according to governance severity.

Examples include:

- governance approval routing
- compliance review
- supervisory intervention
- manual authorization workflows

Objective:

Ensure high-risk execution remains subject to governance oversight.

---

## 6. Human Governance Review

Escalated workflows may require:

- manual review
- policy confirmation
- governance authorization
- operational intervention

Objective:

Maintain human governance control for high-risk operations.

---

## 7. Governance Resolution

Governance review produces:

- execution approval
- operational restriction
- escalation continuation
- execution denial
- governance intervention

Objective:

Ensure execution remains operationally trustworthy.
