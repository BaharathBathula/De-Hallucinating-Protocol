# Governance Risk Classification Framework

---

# Overview

De-Hallucinating Protocol defines a governance risk classification framework designed to evaluate operational risk severity across enterprise AI execution lifecycles.

The objective of the framework is to classify governance-related operational risk according to:

- trust degradation
- policy exposure
- escalation severity
- operational instability
- regulatory sensitivity
- execution impact

The framework treats governance risk as a continuously evolving runtime condition.

---

# Core Philosophy

Traditional AI systems frequently evaluate outputs using simplified confidence scoring models.

De-Hallucinating Protocol instead prioritizes operational governance risk classification.

The framework assumes operational risk may emerge from:

- governance instability
- hallucination indicators
- policy conflicts
- escalation failures
- retrieval corruption
- authority inconsistencies
- execution misalignment

Risk classification exists to support deterministic governance enforcement.

---

# Governance Risk Dimensions

## 1. Trust Risk

Measures operational trust degradation.

Factors include:

- trust instability
- low retrieval confidence
- governance uncertainty
- contradiction density
- reasoning inconsistency

### Operational Impact

Execution reliability degradation.

---

## 2. Policy Risk

Measures governance policy exposure.

Factors include:

- policy violations
- compliance conflicts
- procedural inconsistency
- governance boundary violations

### Operational Impact

Regulatory and operational exposure.

---

## 3. Escalation Risk

Measures escalation-related operational instability.

Factors include:

- missing escalation
- delayed human review
- escalation bypass
- unresolved governance conflicts

### Operational Impact

Uncontrolled execution risk.

---

## 4. Authority Risk

Measures source and authority instability.

Factors include:

- unverified retrieval sources
- invalid policy references
- authority contamination
- unsupported execution reasoning

### Operational Impact

Trust collapse and execution invalidation.

---

## 5. Execution Risk

Measures operational execution instability.

Factors include:

- workflow divergence
- execution-policy mismatch
- unintended automation
- governance restriction violations

### Operational Impact

Operational disruption and governance failure.

---

# Risk Severity Levels

## Level 1 — Low Risk

Minimal governance exposure.

Execution remains operationally stable.

### Governance Response

Monitoring only.

---

## Level 2 — Moderate Risk

Observable trust degradation or policy instability.

### Governance Response

Elevated observability and restricted execution.

---

## Level 3 — High Risk

Significant governance instability or escalation sensitivity.

### Governance Response

Mandatory escalation evaluation.

---

## Level 4 — Critical Risk

Severe governance failure with regulatory or operational exposure.

### Governance Response

Immediate execution restriction and governance intervention.

---

## Level 5 — Catastrophic Risk

Uncontrolled execution conditions capable of producing systemic operational impact.

### Governance Response

Execution lockdown and incident response activation.

---

# Dynamic Risk Reevaluation

The framework continuously reevaluates operational risk throughout runtime execution.

Risk reevaluation may trigger:

- trust state transitions
- escalation workflows
- execution restrictions
- governance intervention
- operational containment

Risk is treated as a continuously evolving operational condition.

---

# Governance-Oriented Risk Philosophy

The protocol prioritizes operational trustworthiness over unrestricted automation.

Risk classification exists to support:

- bounded execution
- deterministic governance
- escalation-aware orchestration
- runtime trust enforcement
- operational reliability

The objective is controlled enterprise AI execution.

---

# Design Goals

The Governance Risk Classification Framework is designed to support:

- runtime governance enforcement
- operational risk visibility
- escalation prioritization
- deterministic execution control
- governance-aware observability
- regulated AI infrastructure
- hallucination mitigation

---

# Architectural Objective

The objective of the Governance Risk Classification Framework is to transform enterprise AI risk evaluation from static confidence scoring into continuously governable operational risk orchestration.
