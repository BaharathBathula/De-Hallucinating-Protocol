# Enterprise AI Trust Lifecycle

---

# Overview

De-Hallucinating Protocol defines an enterprise trust lifecycle for governance-aware AI execution.

The trust lifecycle models how enterprise AI systems transition through governance-controlled operational stages during runtime execution.

The objective is to ensure that trustworthiness is continuously validated throughout the execution lifecycle rather than assumed at generation time.

---

# Core Philosophy

Traditional AI systems typically evaluate outputs after generation.

De-Hallucinating Protocol instead treats trust as a continuously evolving operational state.

The protocol assumes that:

- trust can degrade during execution
- governance conditions may change dynamically
- retrieval reliability may fluctuate
- policy conflicts may emerge at runtime
- escalation requirements may evolve continuously

The lifecycle exists to operationalize continuous governance-aware execution.

---

# Trust Lifecycle Stages

## 1. Identity Verification

The lifecycle begins with validation of:

- initiating user identity
- execution authority
- organizational role permissions
- system-level access controls

Objective:

Prevent unauthorized or policy-invalid execution initiation.

---

## 2. Context Establishment

The protocol establishes trusted operational context.

This includes:

- enterprise metadata
- workflow state
- session continuity
- organizational constraints
- runtime operational conditions

Objective:

Prevent context corruption and execution ambiguity.

---

## 3. Grounding Validation

The protocol validates:

- enterprise knowledge sources
- retrieval consistency
- document provenance
- source authority
- policy reference integrity

Objective:

Reduce hallucination risk caused by untrusted retrieval.

---

## 4. Runtime Validation

The protocol evaluates:

- logical consistency
- contradiction detection
- policy alignment
- reasoning integrity
- operational coherence

Objective:

Ensure execution reliability before governance approval.

---

## 5. Governance Evaluation

The governance engine evaluates:

- policy compliance
- escalation boundaries
- operational restrictions
- execution eligibility
- governance integrity

Objective:

Determine whether execution is governable.

---

## 6. Trust Assessment

The trust engine continuously evaluates:

- governance confidence
- hallucination probability
- runtime risk exposure
- retrieval reliability
- operational trust state

Objective:

Determine runtime execution trustworthiness.

---

## 7. Escalation Orchestration

The protocol determines whether execution requires:

- human review
- governance approval
- operational restriction
- workflow interruption
- escalation routing

Objective:

Ensure high-risk execution remains governable.

---

## 8. Controlled Execution

Execution proceeds only after governance validation succeeds.

Execution may operate under:

- autonomous approval
- monitored execution
- restricted execution
- escalated supervision

Objective:

Enable governance-aware operational execution.

---

## 9. Immutable Audit Logging

The protocol records:

- governance decisions
- trust state transitions
- escalation events
- execution approvals
- runtime anomalies
- policy enforcement actions

Objective:

Maintain audit-grade traceability.

---

# Dynamic Lifecycle Reevaluation

The protocol continuously reevaluates trust throughout execution.

Lifecycle reevaluation may trigger:

- state transitions
- governance restrictions
- escalation workflows
- execution pauses
- runtime intervention

Trust is treated as a dynamic operational condition.

---

# Lifecycle Design Goals

The Enterprise AI Trust Lifecycle is designed to support:

- deterministic governance
- runtime trust orchestration
- hallucination mitigation
- enterprise auditability
- governance-aware execution
- operational reliability
- escalation-controlled AI systems

---

# Architectural Objective

The objective of the Enterprise AI Trust Lifecycle is to transform enterprise AI systems from generation-centric architectures into continuously governed operational systems.
