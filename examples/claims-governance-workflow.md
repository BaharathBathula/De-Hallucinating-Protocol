# Insurance Claims Governance Workflow

---

# Overview

This workflow demonstrates how De-Hallucinating Protocol governs enterprise AI execution during insurance claims processing operations.

The objective is to ensure that AI-assisted claims workflows remain:

- policy-aware
- continuously governable
- audit-traceable
- escalation-controlled
- operationally trustworthy

throughout runtime execution.

---

# Governance Scenario

An enterprise claims processing system receives a customer claim submission requiring AI-assisted evaluation.

The AI system may assist with:

- claims classification
- policy interpretation
- eligibility analysis
- fraud indicators
- workflow recommendations
- escalation prioritization

The protocol governs execution throughout the workflow lifecycle.

---

# Governance Workflow Lifecycle

```text
Claim Submission
        ↓
Identity Verification
        ↓
Policy Retrieval & Grounding
        ↓
Context Validation
        ↓
Claims Reasoning Evaluation
        ↓
Governance Policy Enforcement
        ↓
Runtime Trust Scoring
        ↓
Risk Classification
        ↓
Escalation Evaluation
        ↓
Controlled Recommendation
        ↓
Human Approval (if required)
        ↓
Immutable Audit Logging
```

---

# Workflow Stages

## 1. Claim Submission

The workflow begins when a customer submits a claim request.

Examples include:

- vehicle claims
- property claims
- health claims
- liability claims

### Governance Objective

Ensure claim intake remains traceable and policy-linked.

---

## 2. Identity Verification

The Identity Service validates:

- claimant identity
- access authorization
- workflow ownership
- operational permissions

### Governance Objective

Prevent unauthorized claims execution.

---

## 3. Policy Retrieval & Grounding

The Grounding Service retrieves:

- policy definitions
- coverage rules
- exclusions
- endorsements
- claims procedures

The protocol validates:

- source authority
- document provenance
- policy integrity

### Governance Objective

Reduce hallucinated policy interpretation.

---

## 4. Context Validation

The Context Service validates:

- claim metadata
- customer state
- workflow continuity
- operational consistency

### Governance Objective

Prevent context corruption and execution ambiguity.

---

## 5. Claims Reasoning Evaluation

The Validation Service evaluates:

- reasoning consistency
- policy alignment
- contradiction detection
- execution coherence

### Governance Objective

Prevent invalid claims reasoning.

---

## 6. Governance Policy Enforcement

The Policy Engine evaluates:

- compliance requirements
- claims authority boundaries
- approval requirements
- operational restrictions

### Governance Objective

Ensure policy-aware execution.

---

## 7. Runtime Trust Scoring

The Trust Engine evaluates:

- retrieval integrity
- governance stability
- authority confidence
- operational risk
- escalation necessity

### Governance Objective

Determine execution trustworthiness.

---

## 8. Risk Classification

The Governance Risk Framework classifies operational severity.

Examples include:

- low-risk claims
- policy-sensitive claims
- fraud-sensitive claims
- regulatory-sensitive claims

### Governance Objective

Determine governance enforcement intensity.

---

## 9. Escalation Evaluation

The Escalation Engine determines whether workflows require:

- manual adjuster review
- supervisory approval
- compliance escalation
- governance intervention

### Governance Objective

Ensure high-risk claims remain governable.

---

## 10. Controlled Recommendation

The system produces governance-aware operational recommendations.

Examples include:

- approve claim
- request additional documentation
- escalate investigation
- restrict execution
- deny autonomous approval

### Governance Objective

Ensure bounded execution behavior.

---

## 11. Human Approval

High-risk claims require:

- adjuster review
- compliance validation
- governance approval
- operational supervision

### Governance Objective

Maintain human governance control.

---

## 12. Immutable Audit Logging

The Audit Engine records:

- governance decisions
- trust scores
- escalation events
- execution restrictions
- approval history
- operational anomalies

### Governance Objective

Ensure audit-grade traceability.
