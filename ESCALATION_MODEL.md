# Deterministic Escalation Model

---

# Overview

De-Hallucinating Protocol defines a deterministic escalation model designed to ensure that high-risk enterprise AI execution remains continuously governable through structured human oversight and governance-aware intervention.

The objective of the escalation model is to operationalize escalation as a mandatory governance mechanism rather than an optional exception workflow.

The framework treats escalation as a core operational component of trustworthy enterprise AI systems.

---

# Core Philosophy

Traditional AI systems frequently rely on probabilistic confidence thresholds to determine escalation requirements.

De-Hallucinating Protocol instead treats escalation as a governance-driven operational decision.

The protocol assumes escalation may be required due to:

- governance instability
- trust degradation
- policy ambiguity
- regulatory sensitivity
- authority conflicts
- operational risk exposure
- execution uncertainty

Escalation is treated as deterministic governance enforcement rather than reactive exception handling.

---

# Escalation Design Principles

## 1. Governance-Aware Escalation

Escalation decisions must consider:

- governance severity
- policy sensitivity
- compliance exposure
- operational trust states
- execution authority boundaries

Escalation is not based solely on model confidence.

---

## 2. Deterministic Escalation Boundaries

High-risk workflows must define explicit escalation boundaries.

Examples include:

- financial approvals
- claims adjudication
- underwriting recommendations
- regulatory decisions
- policy modifications

Execution must pause automatically when escalation boundaries are triggered.

---

## 3. Human-in-the-Loop Supervision

The framework assumes certain operational decisions should remain subject to human governance oversight.

Escalation workflows may require:

- manual review
- governance approval
- policy confirmation
- supervisory authorization
- operational intervention

The protocol does not assume unrestricted autonomous execution is acceptable in regulated environments.

---

## 4. Runtime Escalation Reevaluation

Escalation requirements may evolve dynamically during runtime execution.

Triggers may include:

- trust degradation
- policy conflicts
- retrieval instability
- governance violations
- operational anomalies

Escalation eligibility is continuously reevaluated throughout execution lifecycles.

---

# Escalation States

## Monitored Escalation

Execution proceeds under elevated governance observability.

---

## Conditional Escalation

Execution proceeds only if predefined governance constraints remain satisfied.

---

## Required Escalation

Execution pauses pending human governance approval.

---

## Critical Escalation

Immediate governance intervention required.

Execution restrictions enforced automatically.

---

Escalation may trigger due to:

- governance conflicts

- policy ambiguity
- regulatory sensitivity
- financial risk exposure
- operational instability
